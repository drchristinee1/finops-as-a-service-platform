# FinOps Service Portal

## Overview

The FinOps Service Portal is the primary entry point for stakeholders to engage with FinOps capabilities.

It is designed to enable **self-service access**, reduce dependency on centralized FinOps teams, and improve decision-making across engineering, finance, and product teams.

---

## Objectives

- Provide a single, centralized interface for all FinOps services
- Enable stakeholders to access cost insights independently
- Standardize how requests, support, and workflows are handled
- Improve transparency and accountability across teams

---

## Core Capabilities

### 1. Cost Visibility

Stakeholders can:
- Access dashboards for their services, accounts, and environments
- View trends, anomalies, and key cost drivers
- Understand cost behavior over time

---

### 2. Self-Service Requests

Users can submit requests for:
- Cost analysis and investigation
- Optimization recommendations
- Budget and forecast support
- Tagging and allocation corrections

---

### 3. Documentation & Playbooks

The portal provides:
- FinOps best practices
- Optimization playbooks
- Cost driver explanations
- KPI definitions and reporting standards

---

### 4. Workflow Integration

All requests are routed through structured workflows:
- Intake forms (Jira or equivalent)
- Ownership assignment
- SLA tracking
- Escalation paths

---

### 5. Feedback Loop

The portal integrates feedback mechanisms to improve FinOps operations:
- Request resolution tracking
- Stakeholder satisfaction
- Recurring issue identification
- Continuous improvement cycles

---

## Operating Model

Detection → Insight → Request → Action → Optimization → Feedback

---

## Value Delivered

By implementing a FinOps Service Portal, organizations can:

- Scale FinOps across multiple teams and environments
- Reduce manual intervention from FinOps analysts
- Enable faster, data-driven decision-making
- Improve cost accountability and ownership
