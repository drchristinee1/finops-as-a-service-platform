# Cost Variance Narrative Template

## Overview

This template standardizes how cloud cost variances are communicated to stakeholders.

It ensures that cost changes are clearly explained, tied to underlying drivers, and translated into actionable next steps.

---

## 1. Summary

Provide a concise overview of the variance.

- Time period:
- Service / Application:
- Variance amount:
- Variance percentage:

**Example:**  
Cloud spend increased by 18% week-over-week for the Payments service, driven primarily by increased compute usage.

---

## 2. What Happened

Describe the observed change in cost.

- Which services increased or decreased
- When the change occurred
- Magnitude of the change

---

## 3. Why It Happened (Cost Drivers)

Explain the root cause using driver-based analysis.

- Usage increase (e.g., traffic growth, batch jobs)
- Configuration changes (instance size, scaling behavior)
- New deployments or features
- Pricing changes or commitment coverage shifts

---

## 4. Impact

Explain the business or technical impact.

- Budget implications
- Impact on forecast accuracy
- Cost per unit changes (if applicable)
- Sustainability of the cost increase

---

## 5. Actions Taken / Recommended

Define clear next steps.

- Immediate actions (e.g., rightsizing, cleanup)
- Medium-term improvements (e.g., architecture changes)
- Monitoring or guardrails to prevent recurrence

---

## 6. Owner

Identify the responsible team or individual.

---

## 7. Status

Track progress of the response.

- Open
- In Progress
- Resolved
- Accepted (if cost is expected and justified)

---

## 8. Follow-Up

Define next checkpoints.

- Review date
- KPI tracking (time-to-detect, time-to-act)
- Validation of expected outcomes

---

## Value of This Template

This approach ensures:

- Clear communication between FinOps, engineering, and finance
- Consistent explanation of cost changes
- Faster decision-making
- Stronger accountability and ownership
